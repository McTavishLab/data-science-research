The course data science research in biology aims to provide students with best research computing skills and practices.

This hands-on course will cover basic concepts and tools for data science in biology, including statistical design, version control, data management, task automation, and open science.

Students will be encouraged to help one another along the course and to apply what they have learned to a research problem.

Some activities that will be covered are:

  • Basics of scientific software applications,
  • creating automatic reproducible reports,
  • developing and maintaining academic project websites.

For more information on what we will teach in this course and why, please see the following papers: "Best Practices for Scientific Computing", "Continual Update of Phylogenetic trees", .
